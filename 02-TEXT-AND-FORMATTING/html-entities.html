HTML Entities and Special Characters

What are HTML Entities?

HTML entities are used to display special characters that have special meaning in HTML or characters that are not easily typed on a keyboard.

Non-Breaking Space

Regular spaces can break at line endings, but non-breaking spaces keep words together.

Entity: &nbsp; or &#160;

Currency Symbols

  • Dollar: $ (&#36;)
  • Euro: € (&euro; or &#8364;)
  • Pound: £ (&pound; or &#163;)
  • Yen: ¥ (&yen; or &#165;)
  • Cent: ¢ (&cent; or &#162;)

Copyright and Legal Symbols

  • Copyright: © (&copy; or &#169;)
  • Registered: ® (&reg; or &#174;)
  • Trademark: ™ (&trade; or &#8482;)
  • Section: § (&sect; or &#167;)
  • Paragraph: ¶ (&para; or &#182;)

Mathematical Symbols

  • Plus-minus: ± (&plusmn; or &#177;)
  • Multiplication: × (&times; or &#215;)
  • Division: ÷ (&divide; or &#247;)
  • Not equal: ≠ (&ne; or &#8800;)
  • Less than or equal: ≤ (&le; or &#8804;)
  • Greater than or equal: ≥ (&ge; or &#8805;)

Arrows and Symbols

  • Left arrow: ← (&larr; or &#8592;)
  • Right arrow: → (&rarr; or &#8594;)
  • Up arrow: ↑ (&uarr; or &#8593;)
  • Down arrow: ↓ (&darr; or &#8595;)
  • Heart: ♥ (&hearts; or &#9829;)
  • Spade: ♠ (&spades; or &#9824;)

Accented Characters

  • Á á (&Aacute; &aacute;)
  • É é (&Eacute; &eacute;)
  • Í í (&Iacute; &iacute;)
  • Ó ó (&Oacute; &oacute;)
  • Ú ú (&Uacute; &uacute;)
  • ñ (&ntilde;)

Entity Format Types

  1. Named entities: &name; (e.g., &copy;)
  2. Decimal entities: &#number; (e.g., &#169;)
  3. Hexadecimal entities: &#xnumber; (e.g., &#xA9;)

Note: Always end entities with a semicolon (;)
